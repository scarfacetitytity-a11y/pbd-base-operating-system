# trading/trading_framework.md

## Trading Identity

The trader is building toward discipline, funded trading, algorithmic trading, and long-term consistency.

Main trading instruments:
- XAUUSD / Gold
- US30
- US100
- GBPUSD
- Forex and indices

Main problems to solve:
- Overtrading
- Revenge trading
- Entering too early
- Trading emotionally
- Moving stops
- Chasing productivity through trades
- Not waiting for high-quality setups
- Letting friends or outside voices influence execution
- Accidental entries/pocket trades
- Not reviewing trades before entry

Main trading philosophy:
- Identity over outcomes
- Patience and restraint are the edge
- Boredom and inactivity often mean correct execution
- No-trade days count as wins
- Capital protection comes first
- Rule adherence matters more than PnL
- Consistency over talent
- Do not force trades just to feel productive

## Core Trading Model: Sniper v1.0

The Sniper model uses:

1. Higher timeframe context
   - M30 / M15 premium or discount zone
   - Clear directional bias
   - Avoid trading in the middle of range

2. M5 structure confirmation
   - Break of structure
   - Market shift
   - Liquidity sweep confirmation

3. M1 entry confirmation
   - Rejection candle
   - Liquidity grab
   - Entry only after confirmation
   - Avoid guessing before confirmation

4. Risk
   - Risk should be controlled
   - Favour A-star setups
   - Preferred target structure: clean risk-to-reward, often 1:2 or better
   - Never increase risk emotionally

## Pre-entry Checklist

Before any trade:
- What is the higher timeframe bias?
- Am I in premium or discount?
- Has liquidity been swept?
- Is there M5 structure confirmation?
- Is there M1 confirmation?
- Where is invalidation?
- Where is TP?
- Is the trade worth the risk?
- Am I calm or emotional?
- Am I forcing this?

If the answer is unclear, do not trade.
